Anand no Garbo — Evening Celebration
The wedding festivities begin with the sacred and joyful tradition of Garba — a devotional dance form dedicated to Goddess Amba. Family and friends come together to celebrate with music, colour and spiritual energy as we mark the beginning of this auspicious occasion.
6:00 PM onwards
🌺
Welcome & Gathering
Guests arrive and are welcomed with traditional floral decorations, diyas and the fragrance of fresh marigolds. Traditional Gujarati snacks and welcome drinks are served.
Traditional Gujarati welcome snacks served
Decorative diya lighting ceremony
Family photo sessions at decorated mandap

7:00 PM
🙏
Aarti & Mantra Chanting
The evening begins with a sacred aarti performed by the family pandit, followed by group chanting of Amba Mata's prayers to invoke blessings for the upcoming wedding.
Shri Amba Chalisa recitation
Collective aarti with bells and dhol
Prasad distribution to all guests

Live dhol & folk music performance

02
Day Two
Sunday · 25 January 2027
Rituals, Blessings & Music Night
Sacred ceremonies by day, unforgettable music by night
7:00 AM
🌅
Morning Puja & Sankalp
The day begins with a sacred morning puja. The family priest performs the Sankalp — a formal declaration and commitment for the wedding rituals to follow, setting the spiritual intention for all ceremonies.
Surya Namaskar and morning prayer
Sankalp — priest-led intention setting ceremony
Holy water (Gangajal) purification ritual
8:30 AM
🐘
Ganesh Puja
All auspicious Hindu ceremonies begin with Ganesh Puja — worshipping Lord Ganesha, the remover of obstacles, to bless the wedding with smooth proceedings and eternal happiness.
Sthapana of Lord Ganesh idol with flowers & sweets
16 Upachar (16 steps of worship) performed by family
Modak and laddoo offering — Ganesh's favourite
Ganesh Aarti sung by all family members
Distribution of prasad to all attendees
10:00 AM
🏠
Grah Shanti Puja
Grah Shanti is a powerful Vedic ritual performed to pacify the nine planets (Navagrahas) and seek their blessings for a prosperous and harmonious married life. The ceremony creates a spiritually charged environment for the wedding.
Navagraha mandal preparation with 9 sacred colours
Vedic mantras chanted for each of the 9 planets
Havan kund (sacred fire) with specific wood and herbs
Specific ahutis (offerings) for each planet
Special prayers to pacify Shani, Rahu and Ketu
Priest performs aarti and blesses the couple
12:30 PM
☀️
Lunch Break & Family Bonding
A relaxed family lunch bringing both sides of the family together. Time for informal bonding, stories and laughter before the afternoon ceremonies.
Traditional home-cooked Gujarati lunch
Family introduction ceremonies (both sides)
Gift exchange between families
2:00 PM
💛
Haldi Ceremony
One of the most joyful and colourful pre-wedding rituals. Turmeric paste mixed with sandalwood and rose water is applied to the bride and groom by family members to purify, beautify and bless them before their wedding.
Fresh turmeric paste prepared by the ladies of the house
Groom's haldi applied first by mother and sisters
Friends and cousins join in with playful yellow colours
Folk songs (haldi geet) sung throughout
Flower petal shower on the groom
Photography & video coverage
4:30 PM
🌸
Aland (Pithi) Ceremony
Aland (also called Pithi) is a traditional Gujarati ritual where a paste of chickpea flour, turmeric, rose water and sandalwood is applied to the groom for skin brightening and as a symbol of purification before the wedding day.
Pithi paste prepared by the married women of family
Applied on face, arms, neck and feet
Groom sits on a decorated wooden paat (plank)
Mangal geet (auspicious songs) sung by family ladies
Protection thread (raksha) tied on groom's wrist
Ceremony followed by a ritual bath with herbal water
7:00 PM
🎵
Sangeet & Music Night
The night comes alive with a grand music and dance celebration! Family and friends perform choreographed dance acts, sing bollywood hits and celebrate Shiv with heartfelt performances.
Choreographed dance performances by family groups
Solo and duet musical performances
Bollywood hits & Gujarati folk fusion DJ set
Open dance floor for all guests
Special performance by the groom's friends
Photo booth with fun wedding props
Midnight snacks and beverages
03
Day Three
Monday · 26 January 2027 · Republic Day
Jaan to Vadu — The Grand Wedding
The most auspicious day · The union of two souls
💍
The Grand Wedding Day
The most sacred and celebrated day of the three-day festivities. The groom's procession — the Jaan — departs from Amitnagar and arrives at Krishna Valley where the bride and her family await. The day brings together ancient Vedic rituals, heartfelt vows and the joyful union of two families. Being on Republic Day makes this day doubly special — a day of national pride and personal commitment.
6:00 AM
🌄
Groom's Morning Rituals
The wedding morning begins with special prayers, a ritual bath with herbal and flower water, and the groom being dressed in traditional wedding attire by his mother.
Sacred herbal bath with rose water and milk
Wearing of traditional sherwani and safa (turban)
Mother's special aarti and blessings
Sehra (floral veil) tying ceremony
📍 Amitnagar
10:00 AM
🐴
Jaan — Groom's Procession
The most vibrant part of the wedding — the Jaan! The groom leads a grand procession of family and friends with dhol, shehnai, fireworks and dancing. The baraat marches joyfully from Amitnagar towards Krishna Valley.
Groom arrives on decorated horse / classic car
Live dhol-tasha band leading the procession
Fireworks and flower petals along the route
All family and friends dance in the baraat
Procession from Amitnagar to Krishna Valley
Grand Procession
📍 Amitnagar → Krishna Valley
11:30 AM
🌺
Vadu Pakhena — Bride's Welcome
The bride's family performs the traditional welcome of the groom at the wedding venue. The mother of the bride performs aarti of the groom and formally invites the Jaan party inside.
Dwaar Puja — prayers at the entrance gate
Bride's mother performs groom's aarti
Jaimala — flower garland exchange between bride and groom
Welcome of Jaan party with flowers and sweets
📍 Krishna Valley Entrance
12:00 PM
🔥
Vivah Vidhi — Wedding Ceremony
The most sacred part — the Vedic wedding ceremony performed by the family priest. Ancient Sanskrit mantras are chanted as the couple takes their vows around the sacred fire (Agni).
Kanyadaan — father gives away the bride
Hasta Milap — joining of hands
Mangalsutra tying ceremony
Sindoor ceremony
Saptapadi — 7 sacred vows around the holy fire
Ashirvad — blessings from all elders
Main Ceremony
📍 Main Mandap · Krishna Valley
2:30 PM
🍽️
Grand Wedding Feast
A magnificent wedding feast for all guests — a spread of over 50 dishes featuring royal Gujarati, Rajasthani and Indian cuisine served with warmth and celebration.
Full royal thali with 50+ items
Live pav bhaji, pizza and chaat counters
Dessert counter — gulab jamun, ice cream, halwa
Welcome mocktails and fresh juices
📍 Banquet Hall · Krishna Valley

🥲
Vidaai — Farewell Ceremony
The most emotional moment of the wedding — the Vidaai. The bride bids farewell to her family home, throwing back handfuls of rice as she leaves, symbolizing her gratitude and prayers for her family's prosperity.
Rice throwing ritual by the bride
Final blessings from all elders
Decorated car send-off for the couple
Group blessings and flower shower
